Abhinandanism: The Vedic Crown

An Ideology for the New Age, Rooted in Ancient Wisdom.

What is Abhinandanism?

Abhinandanism or Dominionism is a Third Positionist political ideology that synthesizes elements of patriotic nationalism, militarism, and civilizational revivalism rooted in Sanatan Dharma. It advocates for a centralized, authoritarianform of governance led by a morally upright and strong military leader, with the core aim of restoring India’s historical and spiritual greatness. The ideology positions itself as a post-democratic, post-colonial response to perceived internal divisions and external threats to national sovereignty and cultural integrity. It combines cultural traditionalism with statist authoritarianism, rejecting both liberal democratic pluralism and Marxist collectivism. The ideology promotes the awakening of civilizational consciousness, encouraging individuals to align personal growth with national and cultural resurgence.

Key Features of Abhinandanism (in Political Science Terms):

  • Authoritarian Militarism: Advocates for a military-led state apparatus, wherein the armed forces act as the guarantors of national unity, integrity, and discipline. Civilian leadership is subordinated to a singular commander-statesman role combining executive and symbolic authority.
  • Cultural Traditionalism and Civilizational Nationalism: Emphasizes the primacy of Sanatan Dharma as a cultural and ethical foundation for national life. Seeks to revive Indigenous traditions, rejecting imported ideologies, religions, and colonial social constructs.
  • Religious Exclusionism: Defines Indian identity in ethnocultural-religious terms, asserting that non-indigenous religions (Islam, Christianity) are incompatible with the national ethos. Supports a Hindu-first civilizational framework, wherein religious minorities are either assimilated or politically marginalized.
  • Vedic+Finnish Education System: Students would progress when they master knowledge, not when they beat classmates. Vedic+Finnish Education System provides the best ground for growth, education and also, emotional well-being.

Position of Abhinandanism in the political spectrum:

  • Authoritarian Right: Strong state power, hierarchical order, cultural supremacy.
  • Third Positionist:Rejects both left-wing internationalism and right-wing liberal capitalism.
  • Civilisational Statism:Views the state as an embodiment of national spirit rather than a contract-based liberal institution.
